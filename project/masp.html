MASP: Art and Architecture on Paulista Avenue

Welcome to MASP!

Suspended above Paulista Avenue by four massive red pillars, the Museu de Arte de São Paulo is as famous for its radical architecture as it is for its world-class art collection. Designed by Lina Bo Bardi, this institution is a landmark of modernism and a cultural heart of the city.

This guide will walk you through its incredible collection, from Renaissance masters to contemporary Brazilian artists, and explain the genius behind its unique exhibition design. Let's dive in!

The Revolutionary Crystal Easels

One of MASP's most groundbreaking features is its exhibition design. Instead of placing art on walls, Lina Bo Bardi created "cavaletes de cristal" (crystal easels). These glass and concrete stands arrange the artworks chronologically in an open floor plan, allowing visitors to create their own path through art history. This non-hierarchical display provides a unique, transparent, and immersive experience, challenging the traditional way of viewing art.

A Collection Spanning Hemispheres

The museum holds the most important collection of European art in the Southern Hemisphere. You can stand face-to-face with masterpieces by artists like Rembrandt, Van Gogh, Monet, Renoir, Degas, and Picasso. Beyond the European masters, MASP has a formidable collection of Brazilian art, showcasing the country's artistic evolution from the colonial period to the present day. The collection also includes significant African and Asian art, offering a truly global perspective.

Visitor Information

Opening Hours: Tuesdays: 10 AM - 8 PM. Wednesday-Sunday: 10 AM - 6 PM. (Closed Mondays).
Entrance: Admission is charged, but entry is free for everyone on Tuesdays.
Getting There: The museum is located directly in front of the Trianon-MASP metro station (Line 2 - Green).
Pro Tip: Don't forget to check out the "Vão Livre," the 74-meter-long open plaza beneath the museum, which hosts antique fairs, protests, and public gatherings.
